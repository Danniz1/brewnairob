Why We Pay Above Fair-Trade Prices for Our Beans

A breakdown of where your KSh 420 latte actually goes, and why direct trade matters more than a sticker on the bag.

"Fair trade" certification sets a price floor, but it doesn't always reflect what it actually costs to grow good coffee sustainably. We work directly with our eleven partner farms instead, which lets us pay based on quality and need rather than a flat certified minimum.

Where the money actually goes

On average, a roaster buying through a standard export chain might pay 40-50% of the final retail price back to the farm. By buying directly and cutting out two or three middlemen, we're able to return closer to 65-70% of what we pay for green beans straight to the farms that grew them.

It's not charity — it's better coffee

Farms with stable income reinvest in better processing infrastructure: proper drying beds, consistent washing stations, better-maintained trees. That directly improves cup quality year over year, which is ultimately why we do this — not just out of principle, but because it makes the coffee better.
