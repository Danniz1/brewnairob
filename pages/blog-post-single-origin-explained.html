Decoding the Menu: What "Single Origin" Actually Means

Single origin, single estate, micro-lot — we untangle the terms baristas throw around so you can order with confidence.

These terms get used loosely across the industry, which makes ordering confusing. Here's how we actually define them on our menu.

Single origin

Coffee from one country, sometimes one region — but not necessarily one farm. A bag labelled "Kenya AA" could be a blend of several farms' harvests from the same growing region. It tells you where the coffee is from, not exactly whose hands grew it.

Single estate

One step more specific — coffee from a single, named farm. Our Nyeri AA, for instance, is sourced from a defined group of eleven estates we work with directly, and we can usually tell you which specific farm a particular batch came from.

Micro-lot

The most specific tier: a small, separated batch from a single farm, often processed differently than the rest of that farm's harvest to highlight something unique — a particular plot, a rare varietal, or an experimental fermentation process. These are usually limited and rotate seasonally on our pour-over bar.

Why it matters when you order

The more specific the label, the more consistent and traceable the flavour. If you loved a coffee last visit and want it again, ask your barista for the estate or micro-lot name, not just "the Kenyan one" — that's the detail that'll actually get you the same cup twice.
