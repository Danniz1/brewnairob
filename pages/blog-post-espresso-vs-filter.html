Espresso vs Filter Coffee: What's the Real Difference?

It's not just the machine. Extraction time, pressure, and grind size completely change how the same bean tastes in your cup.

Pour the exact same beans through an espresso machine and a V60, and you'll get two completely different drinks. Neither is more "correct" — they're just extracting different things from the same coffee.

Pressure changes everything

Espresso forces hot water through finely-ground coffee at roughly 9 bars of pressure in under 30 seconds. That speed and force pull out oils and fine particulates that filter brewing leaves behind, which is why espresso has that thick, syrupy body and crema on top.

Filter coffee plays a longer game

Filter methods — V60, Aeropress, drip — rely on gravity alone over several minutes. The paper filter traps oils and sediment, leaving a lighter-bodied, cleaner cup that tends to show more of the coffee's acidity and individual flavour notes.

So which should you order?

If you want something rich and concentrated to sip slowly, or as the base for a milk drink, go espresso. If you want to actually taste the difference between our Nyeri and Kirinyaga lots side by side, filter coffee will show you that far more clearly.
