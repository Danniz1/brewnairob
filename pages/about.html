Our Story

From a small cart in Westlands to Nairobi's most-loved specialty café

How it started

A cup that changed everything

In 2019, our founder Amara Wanjiku returned from a coffee tasting trip in Yirgacheffe, Ethiopia with one big question: why were Kenyan highland beans — some of the finest in the world — being exported while Nairobi's cafés served mediocre commodity blends?

She started BrewNairobi from a small push-cart outside the Westlands Sarit Centre with a hand-grinder, a V60, and a single 5kg bag of Nyeri AA. The queue stretched to the car park by week two.

Today we occupy a 120-seat space in Kilimani, source directly from eleven farms across Nyeri, Kirinyaga, and Murang'a, roast every batch in-house on a Giesen W15, and train the next generation of Kenyan baristas.

What drives us

Our core values

Farmer first

We pay a minimum of 30% above the Nairobi Coffee Exchange floor price to every farm we partner with. Their livelihood is our priority.

Relentless quality

Every lot is cupped before purchase. We reject anything scoring below 84 on the SCA scale — even if it means a shorter menu.

Community space

BrewNairobi hosts free weekend cuppings, barista workshops for youth, and monthly "creative mornings" open to anyone with an idea.
