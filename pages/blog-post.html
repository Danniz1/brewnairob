How to Brew the Perfect V60 Pour-Over at Home

Our head roaster breaks down water temperature, grind size, and pour technique step by step — no fancy gear required, just attention to detail.

The V60 is the single best way to taste exactly what a coffee bean is capable of. Unlike espresso, there's no pressure forcing water through — just gravity, a paper filter, and your patience. That simplicity is exactly why it rewards careful technique.

What you'll need

A V60 dripper, V60 filter papers, a gooseneck kettle (or any kettle you can pour slowly with), a scale, and freshly ground coffee — ideally ground no more than 15 minutes before brewing.

The ratio that matters most

Start with 1 gram of coffee for every 16 grams of water. For a single cup, that's roughly 20g of coffee to 320g of water. Water temperature should sit between 92°C and 96°C — just off the boil.

The pour technique

Begin with a small "bloom" pour — just enough water to saturate the grounds, about double their weight — and let it sit for 30 seconds. This releases trapped CO2 and prepares the coffee for even extraction. Then pour in slow, controlled circles, keeping the water level steady rather than letting it drop and spike.

Total brew time should land between 2:30 and 3:30 minutes. Too fast, and the coffee will taste sour and underdeveloped. Too slow, and it turns bitter and over-extracted. If your time is off, adjust your grind size — finer to slow it down, coarser to speed it up — rather than changing your pour speed.
